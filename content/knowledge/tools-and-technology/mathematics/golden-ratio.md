# Golden Ratio (φ)

The **golden ratio**, denoted by the Greek letter **φ (phi)**, is an irrational mathematical constant approximately equal to **1.618033988749...**. This special number has captivated mathematicians, artists, architects, and naturalists for millennia due to its unique mathematical properties and frequent appearance in nature, art, and design.

## Mathematical Definition

The golden ratio is defined by the equation:

**φ = (1 + √5) / 2 ≈ 1.618**

### Key Properties

1. **Self-Similarity**: φ² = φ + 1
2. **Reciprocal Relationship**: 1/φ = φ - 1 ≈ 0.618
3. **Continued Fraction**: φ = 1 + 1/(1 + 1/(1 + 1/(1 + ...)))
4. **Infinite Series**: φ appears in the convergence of the Fibonacci sequence ratios

## Geometric Construction

### Golden Rectangle
- A rectangle whose sides are in the golden ratio (long side:short side = φ:1)
- Removing a square from a golden rectangle leaves another golden rectangle
- This property creates a logarithmic spiral when connecting the vertices

### Golden Triangle
- Isosceles triangle where the ratio of the side to the base is φ
- Contains angles of 72°, 72°, and 36°
- Forms the basis of regular pentagon geometry

## Relationship with Fibonacci Sequence

The golden ratio is intimately connected to the [[knowledge/tools-and-technology/mathematics/fibonacci-sequences|Fibonacci sequence]]:

- The ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers converges to φ
- F(n+1) / F(n) → φ as n approaches infinity
- This relationship appears in many natural growth patterns

## Occurrences in Nature

### Botany
- **Phyllotaxis**: Arrangement of leaves on stems often follows golden angle (137.5°)
- **Seed heads**: Sunflowers and daisies display spiral patterns in Fibonacci numbers
- **Pine cones**: Spiral arrangements of scales follow Fibonacci patterns
- **Tree branches**: Growth patterns often exhibit golden ratio proportions

### Biology
- **Human body**: Various proportions (e.g., finger bones, facial features) approximate φ
- **DNA**: The DNA molecule measures 34 angstroms by 21 angstroms (Fibonacci numbers)
- **Nautilus shells**: Logarithmic spiral growth follows golden ratio proportions

### Physics
- **Crystal structures**: Some quasicrystals exhibit golden ratio symmetry
- **Quantum mechanics**: The golden ratio appears in certain quantum state descriptions
