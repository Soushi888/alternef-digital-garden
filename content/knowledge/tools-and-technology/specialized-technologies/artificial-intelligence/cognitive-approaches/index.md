## Overview

Cognitive approaches to artificial intelligence draw on models of human thought, reasoning, and problem-solving. Rather than treating AI as purely statistical pattern matching, cognitive approaches attempt to implement structured reasoning, memory, and planning in computational systems.

This area bridges cognitive science, neuroscience, and computer science to build systems that reason more like humans do.

## Topics in This Section

### [[sequential-thinking|Sequential Thinking]]
A structured problem-solving approach where complex tasks are broken down into explicit, ordered reasoning steps. Sequential thinking models mimic deliberate human thought processes, making AI reasoning more transparent and auditable.

### [[homunculus-and-second-brain|Homunculus and Second Brain]]
Two faces of the same drive to externalize and animate cognition: the alchemical familiar and the personal knowledge system as convergent technologies. Traces the historical lineages, applies the Extended Mind thesis, and surfaces a three-stage model (prima materia, animation, direction) for AI-assisted knowledge systems.

### [[telos-being-doing|TELOS as Being, Skills as Doing]]
A two-layer architecture for personal AI: the Being layer (TELOS) holds identity, purpose, vision, and values; the Doing layer (Skills and Agents) holds execution capabilities. Grounds the distinction in Aristotelian teleology and maps it onto the Alchemist-Homunculus correspondence.

A self-analysis of SoushAI as an implementation of the digital homunculus concept: how PAI configuration, the Grimoire, TELOS prima materia, and the Algorithm together constitute a Homunculus that serves as Soushi's laboratory companion in the Great Work.

A philosophical exploration of the Homunculus as interface-membrane rather than interface-window: the membrane/threshold distinction, the neurological homunculus paradox, and how the PAI Grimoire constitutes an active boundary between intention and execution.

## Key Concepts

- **Symbolic AI**: Rule-based systems that reason over explicit symbols and logic
- **Hybrid Systems**: Combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning for structured problem-solving
- **Chain-of-Thought**: Prompting techniques that elicit step-by-step reasoning from language models
- **Working Memory**: Short-term storage in cognitive architectures that influences reasoning
- **Planning**: The ability to sequence actions toward a goal state
