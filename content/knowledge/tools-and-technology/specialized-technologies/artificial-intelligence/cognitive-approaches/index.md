## Overview

Cognitive approaches to artificial intelligence draw on models of human thought, reasoning, and problem-solving. Rather than treating AI as purely statistical pattern matching, cognitive approaches attempt to implement structured reasoning, memory, and planning in computational systems.

This area bridges cognitive science, neuroscience, and computer science to build systems that reason more like humans do.

## Topics in This Section

### [[sequential-thinking|Sequential Thinking]]
A structured problem-solving approach where complex tasks are broken down into explicit, ordered reasoning steps. Sequential thinking models mimic deliberate human thought processes, making AI reasoning more transparent and auditable.

## Key Concepts

- **Symbolic AI**: Rule-based systems that reason over explicit symbols and logic
- **Hybrid Systems**: Combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning for structured problem-solving
- **Chain-of-Thought**: Prompting techniques that elicit step-by-step reasoning from language models
- **Working Memory**: Short-term storage in cognitive architectures that influences reasoning
- **Planning**: The ability to sequence actions toward a goal state
