## Overview

**Biomimetic design** (or biomimicry) draws on 3.8 billion years of evolutionary R&D to solve human design problems. Living systems have solved challenges of resilience, efficiency, communication, and adaptation under constraints that engineering is only beginning to match.

## Core Principles

- **Life creates conditions for life** — rather than simply surviving, living systems regenerate the conditions that sustain them
- **Local adaptation** — solutions are place-specific, not universal
- **Decentralization** — distributed control rather than central authority
- **Feedback loops** — continuous sensing and adjustment rather than predetermined optimization
- **Redundancy and diversity** — resilience through multiple pathways

## Applications in Technology

- **P2P networks**: Model distributed resilience on mycorrhizal networks and ant colony behavior
- **Neural networks**: Inspired by biological neural architecture
- **Swarm algorithms**: Optimization via ant colony and particle swarm approaches
- **Self-healing materials**: Inspired by biological tissue repair
- **Holochain**: Agent-centric design explicitly modeled on biological agent behavior
