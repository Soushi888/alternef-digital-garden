## **What is a Wiki?**  

A **wiki** is a **collaborative, web-based system** that allows users to create, edit, and organize content collectively. Wikis are designed to be **easily editable**, making them ideal for knowledge management, documentation, and community-driven content creation.  

### **Key Features of a Wiki**  

1. **Collaborative Editing** ‚Äì Multiple users can contribute, modify, and improve content in real time.  
2. **Version Control** ‚Äì Changes are tracked, allowing users to revert to previous versions if needed.  
3. **Interlinking** ‚Äì Pages can be linked together to create an interconnected knowledge base.  
4. **Open or Restricted Access** ‚Äì Wikis can be **public** (open to everyone) or **private** (restricted to specific users).  
5. **Structured & Unstructured Content** ‚Äì While some wikis follow structured hierarchies, most allow for flexible content organization through categories and tags.

### **Use Cases of Wikis**  

- **Knowledge Management** ‚Äì Companies and communities use wikis to document processes and share knowledge.  
- **Open Collaboration** ‚Äì Wikis facilitate teamwork and content co-creation.  
- **Personal Knowledge Bases** ‚Äì Individuals use private wikis for research, notes, and digital gardening.
